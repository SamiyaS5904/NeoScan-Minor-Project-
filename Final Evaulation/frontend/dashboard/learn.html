Understanding Jaundice

Explore the science behind neonatal jaundice, clinical risk levels, and how NeoScan AI ensures accurate, non-invasive screening.

What is Neonatal Jaundice?

Neonatal jaundice is a highly common condition affecting newborns, characterized by a yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes. It occurs when there is a buildup of bilirubin in the baby's blood.


Bilirubin is a yellow pigment produced during the normal breakdown of red blood cells. Because a newborn's liver is still immature, it often cannot process and remove bilirubin quickly enough. While usually harmless, excessively high levels can lead to severe complications like Kernicterus (permanent brain damage).

Visible yellowing of the skin is the primary physical indicator of jaundice.

Clinical Risk Levels (Bhutani Nomogram)

Pediatricians assess the severity of jaundice by comparing the infant's age (in hours) against their Total Serum Bilirubin (TSB) levels. This creates distinct risk zones. NeoScan AI utilizes these standard clinical zones to provide actionable insights.

The Bhutani Nomogram: Hours of age vs Total Serum Bilirubin (mg/dL)

Low Risk Zone

Bilirubin levels are within safe physiological limits. Routine monitoring is sufficient.

Intermediate Risk

Levels are elevated. Frequent monitoring is required to ensure levels do not spike.

High Risk Zone

Immediate clinical intervention (such as phototherapy) is strongly recommended.

Our AI Methodology

NeoScan replaces subjective visual estimations with objective, data-driven computer vision. Here is how our underlying technology guarantees high accuracy across different environments.

01

Gray World Light Calibration

Smartphone cameras have different sensors, and home lighting can distort skin color. We utilize the Gray World Algorithm to neutralize ambient color temperature, standardizing the image before analysis.

02

Skin ROI Extraction

The system intelligently crops out backgrounds, clothing, and shadows to isolate the Region of Interest (ROI)—the pure skin pixels—to ensure noise-free data extraction.

03

Random Forest Prediction Model

Features like RGB ratios and YCbCr luminance are extracted and fed into a Random Forest Regression model. Trained on verified clinical datasets, it outputs an estimated Total Serum Bilirubin score with 92%+ correlation to lab tests.
