Medical Knowledge

What is Jaundice?

Jaundice is a condition where the skin and the whites of the eyes (sclera) take on a yellowish tint. It is caused by the accumulation of bilirubin, a yellow pigment produced during the normal breakdown of red blood cells.

Visible Symptoms

  • • Yellowing of the eye whites (Sclera)
  • • Yellowish skin tone starting from the face
  • • Dark, tea-colored urine

Pediatric Context

What is Neonatal Jaundice?

Neonatal jaundice occurs when a newborn has a high level of bilirubin in the blood. Because a baby's immature liver often cannot remove bilirubin quickly enough, it builds up in the body during the first week of life.

If left untreated, extremely high levels can lead to Kernicterus, a type of permanent brain damage.
